## Proposed Conventions

### C11 Bucket-by-Recommendation in Defect Synthesis

**Why:** A "list all 76 findings" Defect Synthesis fights the template's
one-screen constraint. Bucketing by recommendation (fix before porting
/ port differently / leave behind) gives porting authors an actionable
view: "which decisions do I need to make, and what's the recommended
default?" The full catalog is one click away in the underlying defect
reports.

**How to apply:** Defect Synthesis section should:
1. List all critical findings (always).
2. List the highest-impact ~20-30 high findings.
3. End with a count summary: "X fix before porting, Y port differently, Z leave behind. Full row-by-row in `<defect-reports>`."

### C12 Cross-Phase Corrections Applied at Synthesis Boundaries

**Why:** When a later phase (e.g., defect-scan-semantic) finds an error
in an earlier phase's framing (e.g., the architecture phase had the
project-RCE perimeter wrong), there are three places to apply the
correction: (a) amend the earlier primary, (b) document in the later
primary only, (c) apply at the synthesis boundary. The framework
forbids (a). (b) is honest but leaves prior phases stale-looking. (c)
is the cleanest — the synthesis carries the corrected view forward,
and a brief append in the affected secondaries pins it.

**How to apply:** When carrying corrections (`sem-CR<n>` IDs) to a
synthesis phase, apply them in the synthesis primary (with citation)
AND append a brief correction note to the relevant secondary outputs.
Don't amend the original phase's primary.
